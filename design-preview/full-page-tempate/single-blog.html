Understanding the Chemical Resistance of Vapor Polished PA12 Parts

Vapor polishing is not just about aesthetics. In this guide, we break down how chemical finishing alters the surface layer of PA12 parts to improve solvent resistance and cosmetic stability.

The Physics of Vapor Polishing

Unlike bead blasting (a subtractive mechanical process), vapor polishing uses a controlled solvent atmosphere to briefly reflow the outer polymer layer. The result is a sealed surface with lower porosity and improved chemical performance.

Technical Specifications

Surface roughness (Ra) can drop from 6.3μm to below 0.8μm depending on geometry, exposure time, and solvent control. This directly affects capillary absorption and long-term staining resistance.

Surface Transformation

The reflowed skin changes friction, reflectivity, and permeability. For functional parts, treat polishing as a surface engineering step: verify dimensional drift, thread engagement, and critical sealing interfaces.

Conclusion

Vapor polishing can materially improve resistance against oils, fuels, and cleaning agents—when process parameters are controlled. Always validate against your exact media and duty cycle.
